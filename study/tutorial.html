Main Task

In this study, you will be looking for flaws in charts of data. You will be shown a grid of charts that each have sample data in them. Most of the charts are random data, but one of these will have a flaw. Your task is to select the chart that has the flaw. In some cases, this flaw will be difficult to see, or the random noisy data in the other charts will make many of the other charts look flawed. In those cases, give your best guess.

Chart Types

The same sample data, represented as a scatterplot, histogram, and density plot.

You will see up to three different chart types.

Scatterplots

Scatterplots have a small circle for every single data point.

Histograms

Histograms are charts with several bars in them. The more data that there is in a given region, the taller that region's bar is.

Density plots

Density plots are like histograms, but create smooth shapes that are taller where there is more data.

Flaw Types

You will see one of three types of flaws in each question. You will be told what kind of flaw to look for.

Outliers

For data with outliers, there will be a large amount of data points that are much lower, or much higher, than most of the rest of the data.

The original data on the left, and the data with outliers on the right.

Spikes

For data with spikes, there will be a lot of data points with the exact same value.

The original data on the left, and the data with a spike on the right.

Gaps

For data with gaps, there will be a region where there are no visible data points.

The original data on the left, and the data with a gap on the right.

Sampling

The data are generated by randomly sampling. Even data without flaws can look different from other data, just due to random chance. Your task is to find charts with systematic flaws, not just ones that may look different due to the luck of the draw.
